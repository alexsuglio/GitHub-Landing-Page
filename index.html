About Me

A little bit about myself

I first learned to code the old-school way: break something, figure out why it broke, fix it, then move on until I broke the next thing.

That early learning was fueled by curiosity, trial and error, and a heck of a lot of coffee. Over time, every variable, function, class, and late-night debug session added up, and that build-break-fix loop taught me how to think like an engineer.

Today, I bring that same mindset and passion to GitHub, where I help developers and engineers build whatever they're trying to create. I still tinker constantly and I'm always reading, learning, and chasing what's next.
